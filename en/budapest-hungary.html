Budapest, Hungary

July 27, 2016
Budapest was on our list for a long time and just happen to be on the way from Ukraine to Germany. You can get here from Kyiv by WizzAir - tickets usually cost around 50-60 EUR, but we bought ours in advance and they were just 25 EUR per person.
We arrived in Budapest early in the morning and check-in in our rented apartment was only after 14:00, so we had to wait in a cafe nearby. We also had lunch there
After settling in and a short rest we went for an evening walk on the river bank. Our apartment was almost in the city center right in front of the river.

We lived in the yellow building on the right side.

Chain bridge, it was opened in 1849 and became first permanent bridge across Danube river.

Hungarian Parliament.

On the next day it was raining in the morning. As usual, we decided to go to local city market, it was close to our place. There everything was without surprises. There are two levels in the market. On the first level they sell fruits, vegetables and meat. And on the second level there are some clothing shops and cafes. Also, there is a German discounter supermarket Aldi in the basement of the market building.

Dohány Street Synagogue - it's the largest synagogue in Europe.

Budapest Opera.

Memorial to Soviet heroes who liberated Budapest in World War II.

Shoes on the Danube Bank - memorial created to honor Jews who were killed in World War II.

On the next day the weather was better and we went for a walk again.

Went by the market.

Local trolleybus called "Ikarus". In Ukraine we have the same, but as a regular bus

For a lunch we went to a vegan cafe.

Zero kilometer - from this point the distance to other cities in Hungary is calculated.

Fisherman's Bastion is an architectural complex on the Castle hill in Buda part of the city.
