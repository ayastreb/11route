Vienna, Austria

July 30, 2016
From Budapest to Vienna we got by BlaBlaCar. Our driver turned out to be a ukrainian, driving from western Ukraine to Vienna Our drive was fast and uneventful and we've settled in our apartment once we got there. Like in Zurich, our apartment had automated check-in - there was a safe near to the apartment, and the agency had emailed us a password for the safe a day before. Inside the safe was the key for apartment - and no need to wait for anybody
Our apartment was not far away from amusement park called Prater. The giant ferris wheel, called Riesenrad, was built in 1896. There are lots of attractions, contests and other activities in this park.
On the next day we went to the city center. We started from the City Hall and Rathausplatz. On the days we were in Vienna there was some kind of cinema festival, and there was a giant screen and seats for the free screenings.

From the City Hall we went to Austrian Parliament.

And then to Folksgarten.

Strange sculpture.

There are a lot of free drinking water stands in Vienna - quite handy in the hot weather.

The only modern building in Vienna old city center - Haas House. It is considered controversial, some people like it, another hate it

Also we walked in the city park - Stadtpark. Here, as well as in other parks in Vienna, are a lot of benches.

Vienna Opera. Unfortunately the season was over in August and we didn't get in.

Close to the Opera there is Hotel Sacher, in its cafe they serve original Sacher Cake.

And of course the journey wouldn't be complete without a visit to a local city market We went to the Naschmarkt not far away from the Opera. It's the biggest market in Vienna. The market was here since XVIII century.

For lunch we went to the mediterranean cafe - falafel, humus and pita.

Interesting sign.

There is also a monument to soviet soldiers in Vienna.

And lastly we went to the park of Belveder - Belvederegarten. Belvedere Palace was built as a summer residency of prince Eugene of Savoy's.

There was a heron on the sculpture.

Around the palace was some exhibitions of statues from Chinese horoscope - Dog, Horse, Dragon, etc.

And the concrete Snowman.
